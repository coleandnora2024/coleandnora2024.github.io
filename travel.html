We're very excited for you all to join us in Italy! Currently no one on the guest list is actually from Italy, so here's a guide for everyone on how to get to Villa Giulia (Nora wanted to put a 'let me google that for you' link, Cole protested. First compromise of the marriage).

Villa Giulia

Villa Giulia is a historic Monastery nestled within the Marche hills that has now been turned into a lovely Villa surrounded by greenery and overlooking the Adriatic Sea. The Villa has everything that we will need for our wedding weekend (yay!) including a team that will provide all our food using locally sources produce, a well-stocked bar (don't worry, we checked), olive groves and a swimming pool. It's also walking distance to the ocean (for anyone who doesn't like chlorine) and a short drive away from the historic town of Fano.

Accomodation

We would love for you to join us in the Villa which has a range of beautifully decorated rooms and apartments. The accommodation costs 150 EUR per person for the two nights (Friday through Sunday). We have reserved all of the rooms at the Villa so please ....

Food

One of the reasons we picked this venue is because the food is from their organic and local garden. We will be enjoying serveal meals together throughout the weekend on the terrace, in the garden and overlooking the hills and sea.

Getting there

You night find yourself asking, 'How will I get there?'. Don't worry, we've thought about this. Here is some information that might be useful.

There are couple main airports that are close(ish) to the Villa. Below, we list a few options for the airports, and how you can then get to Fano. We can arrange to pick you up from the Fano train station (10 minute drive to the Villa), or multiple of you are arriving at the same time, we can help coordinate lift sharing. Alternatively, you can of course rent a car to get to the Villa directly. The reception on Friday begins at 5pm CET, with dinner beginning at 7pm CET. Unfortunately, some of the flights that we list below will mean that you might miss part/all of the first nights activities, so the best option might be to travel a day or two before (to treat yourself) and explore one of the nearby cities. If that does not work for you, please let us know in advance so that we can inform the catering staff.

  • Ancona (AOI)
    • .

    Ancona airport is the closest to the Villa and there are direct flights from London Stansted. Unfortunately, there is only one flight a day; the flight on Thursday arrives around 6:00 pm and the one on Friday arrives around 9:00 pm.

    There is a return flight back to London Stansted around 4:30pm on Sunday September 1st, which gives you plenty of time to enjoy brunch with everyone before heading back home.

  • Bologna (BLQ)
    • Bologna airport is another convenient option for getting to Fano, and is a beautiful city for you to explore either before or after the wedding. Flights arrive to Bologna at all hours of the day, from all over Europe, including London, Brussels, and Munich . From Bologna, there is a 2 hour train to Fano that passes through the lovely countryside. From London , there are several options and combinations from British Airways, Ryanair, and easyJet. From Brussels, there are also several options that get you into Fano
